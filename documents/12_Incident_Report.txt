INCIDENT REPORT — HR PORTAL OUTAGE
Date: March 3, 2025
Reported by: Derek Osei (Office Manager, Chicago)
Reviewed by: Anita Sharma (Head of Technology)
Severity: Medium
Status: Resolved



SUMMARY

The HR portal (hr.arrowood.internal) was inaccessible for approximately 3 hours and 40 minutes on the morning of March 3, 2025, between 7:50 AM and 11:30 AM CT. During this window, employees were unable to submit time-off requests, access pay stubs, or complete onboarding forms. No data was lost.



TIMELINE

7:50 AM CT — First report of portal inaccessibility received via #it-support Slack channel (reported by a Chicago employee attempting to submit a vacation request)

8:05 AM CT — IT on-call engineer, Paulo Reyes, begins investigation

8:30 AM CT — Root cause identified: a scheduled maintenance update to the authentication service (Okta integration) introduced a configuration error that broke the login flow for the HR portal specifically

9:15 AM CT — Status update posted in #company-announcements on Slack: "We are aware of the HR portal issue and are working on a fix. Expected resolution by 11 AM."

10:45 AM CT — Fix deployed to staging and verified

11:30 AM CT — Fix deployed to production; portal access restored

11:45 AM CT — All-clear posted in #company-announcements



ROOT CAUSE

A configuration change made during a routine Okta maintenance window incorrectly scoped the SSO redirect URL for the HR portal. The change was applied automatically as part of a scheduled batch update and was not individually reviewed before deployment.



IMPACT

- Approximately 85 employees attempted to access the portal during the outage window
- 12 employees contacted IT support directly
- 3 time-off requests that were in-progress were lost and needed to be resubmitted
- No payroll data or personal employee data was affected
- No client-facing systems were impacted



RESOLUTION

The Okta SSO redirect configuration was corrected by IT engineer Paulo Reyes. A full regression test was conducted on the HR portal before restoring access.



PREVENTIVE MEASURES

The following changes will be implemented by March 31, 2025:

1. All configuration changes to SSO settings will require a second reviewer before being applied to production
2. Automated monitoring will be added for the HR portal login flow, with alerts triggered within 5 minutes of a login failure spike
3. Maintenance updates will be scheduled outside of core business hours (before 7 AM or after 7 PM CT) where possible



LESSONS LEARNED

The outage was contained quickly once identified, and communication to employees was clear and timely. The gap was in the change review process — a quick pre-deployment check would have caught the misconfiguration before it reached production.
