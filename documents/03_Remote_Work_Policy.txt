REMOTE WORK & HYBRID POLICY — ARROWOOD & PARTNERS
Last Updated: March 2025



OVERVIEW

Arrowood & Partners operates on a hybrid model. We believe that in-person collaboration matters, but we also trust employees to do great work from wherever they're most productive.



IN-OFFICE EXPECTATIONS

All full-time employees are expected to be in the office a minimum of 2 days per week. Your team lead or manager may set specific anchor days (shared in-office days) based on project needs. Check with your manager if you are unsure which days your team uses.

Employees on client-facing engagements may have different requirements depending on client expectations and project phase. These will be communicated by your project lead.



WORKING FROM HOME

Employees may work from home on non-anchor days without requiring formal approval. Simply keep your calendar updated and remain reachable during core hours.

If you need to work from home on an anchor day due to illness, a home emergency, or a personal obligation, notify your manager as early as possible.



WORKING FROM ANOTHER CITY OR COUNTRY

Short-term remote work from another location (up to 4 weeks per year) is permitted with manager approval. No HR request is needed, but inform your manager in advance.

Working remotely from another country for more than 4 weeks requires formal approval from HR and the Head of People & Culture. There may be tax and employment law implications depending on the country. Do not make long-term international remote arrangements without prior written approval.



HOME OFFICE SETUP

Arrowood & Partners provides a one-time home office stipend of $600 CAD / $500 USD for employees who work remotely at least 2 days per week. This can be used for:

- A monitor or laptop stand
- Ergonomic chair or desk accessories
- Webcam, headset, or keyboard
- Lighting

Submit receipts through the expense portal within 60 days of purchase. This stipend is available once per employee and does not reset annually.



INTERNET AND PHONE

Employees are responsible for maintaining a reliable internet connection at home. There is no monthly internet reimbursement, but if your role requires frequent video calls or handles sensitive client data, speak with IT about whether a mobile hotspot device can be issued.



COWORKING SPACES

If you find it difficult to work productively from home, you may request access to a coworking space in your city. Arrowood covers up to $150/month for a coworking membership with manager and HR approval. Submit the request through the HR portal.



EXPECTATIONS FOR REMOTE EMPLOYEES

Regardless of where you work, employees are expected to:

- Be available and responsive during core working hours
- Keep their calendar up to date with blocks for focus time, calls, or out-of-office periods
- Attend scheduled team meetings via video with camera on (unless bandwidth is an issue)
- Use company-approved tools for communication and file storage — not personal email or personal cloud drives
