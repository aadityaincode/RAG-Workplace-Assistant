PERFORMANCE REVIEWS & CAREER DEVELOPMENT — ARROWOOD & PARTNERS
Last Updated: January 2025



PERFORMANCE REVIEW CYCLE

Arrowood & Partners conducts formal performance reviews once per year, in January. The review covers performance from the previous calendar year (January–December).

The review process has three parts:

1. Self-Assessment: The employee reflects on their goals, achievements, and areas for growth. The self-assessment form is available in the HR portal starting December 1.

2. Manager Review: The direct manager provides a written assessment and rating based on the employee's performance against their goals and core competencies.

3. Review Conversation: The manager and employee meet to discuss the review together. This meeting should feel like a conversation, not a report card.

Reviews are completed by January 31. Compensation adjustments tied to reviews take effect February 1.



PERFORMANCE RATINGS

Employees are rated on a four-point scale:

- Exceeds Expectations: Consistently goes beyond the requirements of the role, demonstrating exceptional results and impact
- Meets Expectations: Reliably delivers on the requirements of the role; solid, consistent performance
- Partially Meets Expectations: Some areas of strong performance, but improvement is needed in key areas; a development plan will be put in place
- Does Not Meet Expectations: Performance falls significantly short of role requirements; formal action will follow

The majority of ratings fall in the "Meets Expectations" category. "Exceeds Expectations" is meaningful and is not given automatically for tenure or effort alone.



GOAL SETTING

Goals for the coming year are set collaboratively between the employee and manager each February, following the review cycle. Goals should be:

- Clear: Specific enough that both parties agree on what "done" looks like
- Realistic: Achievable within the year given current responsibilities
- Aligned: Connected to team and company priorities

Employees are encouraged to include at least one personal development goal (a skill they want to build) in addition to role-based performance goals.

Goals are recorded in Notion and reviewed at mid-year check-ins.



MID-YEAR CHECK-IN

In addition to the annual review, managers and employees have a formal mid-year check-in in July. This is a lighter-weight conversation focused on:

- Progress against goals set in February
- Any adjustments needed given changing priorities
- Support the employee needs in the second half of the year

There is no formal rating at the mid-year check-in.



PROMOTIONS

Promotions at Arrowood & Partners are merit-based and are considered during the annual review cycle. There is no minimum time-in-role requirement, though most promotions occur after at least 18–24 months in a position.

To be considered for a promotion:
- The employee must be performing at or above the level of the next role
- The manager must submit a promotion recommendation to HR by December 15
- Promotions are reviewed and approved by the Head of People & Culture and the relevant department head

Employees may also express interest in a promotion to their manager at any time — the annual cycle is the formal process, but conversations can happen year-round.



CAREER DEVELOPMENT CONVERSATIONS

Beyond formal reviews, employees are encouraged to have regular development conversations with their manager. At least one conversation per quarter focused specifically on career growth (not just current projects) is recommended.

Topics may include:
- Skills you want to develop
- Roles or responsibilities you want to take on
- Areas where you feel stuck or underchallenged
- Interest in moving to a different team or practice area

HR also offers optional career coaching sessions twice per year for all employees. Book a session through the HR portal.
