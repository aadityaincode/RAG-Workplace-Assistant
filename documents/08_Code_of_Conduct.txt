CODE OF CONDUCT — ARROWOOD & PARTNERS
Last Updated: January 2025



PURPOSE

This Code of Conduct outlines the standards of behavior we expect from everyone at Arrowood & Partners — in our offices, while working remotely, at client sites, and at company events. It applies to all employees, contractors, and vendors representing the company.



RESPECTFUL WORKPLACE

Arrowood & Partners is committed to a workplace where every person is treated with dignity and respect. We do not tolerate harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any kind based on race, ethnicity, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, disability, religion, national origin, or any other characteristic.

This includes:
- Verbal, written, or physical harassment
- Derogatory comments or jokes, including in Slack or email
- Unwanted physical contact
- Exclusionary behavior in meetings or team settings
- Retaliating against someone who raises a concern

If you experience or witness conduct that violates this policy, you are encouraged to report it to your manager, HR, or through the anonymous reporting tool at conduct.arrowood.internal.

All reports are taken seriously and investigated promptly. Retaliation against anyone who makes a good-faith report is strictly prohibited and is itself a conduct violation.



CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict — or appear to conflict — with the interests of the company or our clients.

Examples of conflicts to disclose:
- A close personal relationship with a vendor or client
- A financial interest in a company that Arrowood does business with
- Outside employment or consulting work in the same industry
- Hiring or managing a family member

Disclosure does not automatically mean the situation is a problem — it means it can be reviewed and managed appropriately. Disclose potential conflicts to HR at hr@arrowood.internal.



CONFIDENTIALITY

Employees regularly handle sensitive information — about clients, about colleagues, and about the company's business. This information must be protected.

Do not:
- Share client information with people who are not on the engagement
- Discuss internal projects or financials in public places
- Post about client work on personal social media
- Take company documents to a personal device or personal cloud storage

Confidentiality obligations continue after you leave the company, in accordance with your employment agreement.



USE OF COMPANY RESOURCES

Company equipment, systems, and time should be used primarily for work purposes. Reasonable personal use is acceptable (checking personal email, brief personal calls) but should not interfere with your work or consume significant company time.

You may not use company resources to:
- Run a side business
- Access or distribute inappropriate content
- Engage in any activity that is illegal or violates this Code



SOCIAL MEDIA

Employees are free to maintain personal social media accounts. When posting about work:
- Do not share confidential or client-specific information
- Do not speak on behalf of Arrowood & Partners unless authorized
- Be aware that colleagues, clients, and potential clients may see what you post

If you are contacted by media about the company, direct the inquiry to communications@arrowood.internal.



REPORTING A CONCERN

You can raise a concern through any of the following:

- Your direct manager
- HR: hr@arrowood.internal
- Anonymous reporting tool: conduct.arrowood.internal
- Head of People & Culture: Roberto Fuentes (roberto.fuentes@arrowood.internal)

All concerns are handled with discretion. Anonymous reports are reviewed and acted upon where possible.
