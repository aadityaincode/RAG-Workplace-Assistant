1. 2024 YEAR IN REVIEW

Margaret opened by thanking the team for a strong year. Key highlights from 2024:

- Revenue grew 18% year-over-year, exceeding the target of 14%
- 11 new client engagements started, including 3 in the healthcare sector
- Employee headcount grew from 380 to 430
- Average client satisfaction score: 4.6/5 (up from 4.3 in 2023)
- The Chicago office relocated to a larger space in the West Loop

Areas of improvement called out:
- Internal knowledge management has been inconsistent — teams spending too much time searching for documents (addressed with Nexus launch, see below)
- A few project timelines slipped in Q3; project management process to be strengthened



2. 2025 COMPANY PRIORITIES

James Carver (COO) outlined four priorities for 2025:

Priority 1 — Deepen existing client relationships
Focus on multi-year renewals and expanding scope with current clients rather than chasing net-new logos. Each practice area has a target for client retention above 85%.

Priority 2 — Launch Nexus (internal knowledge platform)
The Nexus platform will go company-wide in Q1. All teams are asked to upload their key documents to the platform by February 28. Anita Sharma's team will provide onboarding sessions the week of January 20.

Priority 3 — Build out the Toronto office
Toronto grew from 15 to 40 people in 2024. The goal is to reach 70 by year-end. Hiring is active across all functions. Employees interested in transferring to Toronto should speak with Roberto Fuentes.

Priority 4 — Operational consistency
Standardizing how projects are scoped, staffed, and closed across all offices. A revised project methodology will be rolled out in Q2.



3. ORGANIZATIONAL CHANGES

Roberto Fuentes announced the following:

- The People & Culture team is adding two new HR Business Partners in Q1 — one supporting Austin/Chicago, one supporting Toronto
- A new Director of Learning & Development role has been posted internally (deadline to apply: January 31)
- The IT team is expanding by 3 people to support Nexus and general infrastructure growth



4. BENEFITS UPDATES (EFFECTIVE JANUARY 2025)

Linda Tran (CFO) summarized the following changes to benefits:

- Wellness allowance increased from $300 to $400 USD / $380 to $500 CAD
- Professional development budget increased from $1,000 to $1,500 USD / $1,300 to $2,000 CAD
- RRSP company contribution for Toronto employees increased from 3% to 4% of base salary
- EAP counseling sessions increased from 6 to 8 per year at no cost

No changes to health insurance premiums for 2025.



5. Q&A HIGHLIGHTS

Q: Will there be a mid-year bonus this year?
A: No mid-year bonus is planned. Annual merit increases effective February 1 remain in place.

Q: Is the company considering a 4-day work week?
A: No formal policy change is planned for 2025. The leadership team is monitoring industry trends and open to revisiting in 2026.

Q: Will Nexus replace Notion?
A: No. Nexus is a search and Q&A tool for finding information. Notion remains the place where documents are written and maintained. Nexus searches across Notion and other document sources.
